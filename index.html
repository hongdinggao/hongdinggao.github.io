I am a Principal Scientist ORCID working at Natural Resources Institute Finland (LUKE).

My main research interests include:

  • Statistical models and computational algorithms in quantitative genetics
  • Software development for variance component estimation and genomic prediction
  • Animal and plant breeding (i.e., using DNA information to predict and select superior plant and animal candidates in early cycles)

I received my BSc in Animal Science from China Agricultural University in 2006 and my PhD in Quantitative Genetics from Aarhus University in 2012. From 2013 to 2017, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Aarhus University and Nordic Cattle Genetic Evaluation NAV. After that, I continued at QGG, Aarhus University, as a researcher and assistant professor until late 2021. More information, s available in my CV here. The best way to reach me is via email.

I lived in Denmark for nearly 11 years and am now based in Helsinki, Finland.
